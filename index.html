Introduction: Problem Definition

Bike-sharing systems need to place the right number of bikes at the right time.

  • Underestimating demand creates bike shortages and customer dissatisfaction.
  • Overestimating demand wastes operational resources.
  • Demand changes by hour, weather, season, holidays, and system availability.

Managerial problem

Operations managers need a data-driven way to forecast hourly bike rental demand.

Importance of the Research

Understanding bike demand helps managers improve:

  • Bicycle allocation across stations and operating periods
  • Staffing and maintenance planning
  • Service reliability during peak demand
  • Cost control during low-demand periods
  • Customer satisfaction through better availability

Research Objective and RQ1

Research Objective

Identify the weather and time-related factors that influence hourly bike rental demand in Seoul.

RQ1

Which weather and time-related factors have the greatest influence on
hourly bike rental demand in Seoul?

H1

Weather conditions and time-related variables significantly affect bike rental demand.

Population

The population is all hourly bike rental observations recorded by the Seoul Bike Sharing system during the study period.

Each observation represents one hour and includes:

  • Rented bike count
  • Date and hour
  • Weather conditions
  • Season and holiday indicators
  • System functioning status

From Records to Data

The raw data is already in a tidy record format:

  • Each row = one hourly observation
  • Each column = one measured variable
  • The dependent variable is Rented Bike Count
  • Independent variables include weather, time, season, holiday, and functioning day variables

Data Wrangling Process

The data preparation process included:

  1. Renaming variables for easier analysis
  2. Converting the Date field to date format
  3. Checking missing values and duplicates
  4. Creating weekday and weekend indicators
  5. Creating rush-hour and peak-hour indicators
  6. Removing non-functioning system hours from the modeling sample

Final Usable Sample Decision

The dataset includes hours when the bike-sharing system was not functioning.

  • Non-functioning hours do not represent normal customer demand.
  • These observations show constrained demand because users could not rent bikes.
  • They were removed for the RQ1 demand model.

Correlation Overview

Temperature, hour, dew point, solar radiation = positively related to demand
Humidity, rainfall, snowfall = negatively related to demand

Temperature and Demand

Demand generally rises with temperature, although extremely high or low demand also depends on hour and season.

Rainfall and Demand

Rainy hours have visibly lower demand, supporting the expectation that poor weather reduces bike usage.

Modeling Approach

Two models were used to evaluate RQ1:

  1. Multiple Linear Regression
    • Interpretable baseline model
    • Useful for understanding linear relationships
  2. Random Forest Regression
    • Captures nonlinear patterns and interactions
    • Useful for identifying the strongest predictors

Model evaluation metrics

RMSE, MAE, and R² were used to compare model performance.

Model Performance

Model RMSE MAE R²
Multiple Linear Regression 395.1 302.8 0.602

Interpretation

The Random Forest model performed substantially better, suggesting that bike demand is affected by nonlinear and interacting factors.

Feature Importance

Temperature, hour, and dew point are the strongest predictors.

Prediction Quality

Predicted values closely follow actual rentals, but extreme demand periods remain harder to predict.

Summary Findings for RQ1

RQ1 asked:
Which weather and time-related factors have the greatest influence on
hourly bike rental demand?

Key findings

  • Temperature is the strongest predictor of demand.
  • Hour of day is also highly influential, especially evening commute hours.
  • Seasonality matters: summer and autumn have higher demand than winter.
  • Rainfall, snowfall, and humidity are associated with lower demand.
  • Nonlinear modeling performs better than a simple linear model.

Business Implications

For Seoul Bike Sharing operations managers:

  • Increase bike availability during high-demand evening hours.
  • Plan more resources during warmer seasons, especially summer and autumn.
  • Reduce expected allocation during rain, snow, or high-humidity periods.
  • Use predictive models to support staffing and maintenance scheduling.
  • Monitor weather forecasts as part of daily operational planning.

Limitations

This analysis has several limitations:

  • The dataset is hourly and does not identify individual stations.
  • Station-level redistribution decisions cannot be directly modeled.
  • The model does not include external events, transit disruptions, or tourism patterns.
  • Random Forest improves prediction but is less interpretable than linear regression.

Conclusion

The analysis shows that Seoul bike rental demand is strongly influenced by temperature, hour of day, season, and weather conditions.

The findings support a data-driven allocation strategy where managers adjust bike supply based on expected demand patterns rather than relying on fixed schedules.

Final takeaway

Predictive demand analytics can improve service reliability, reduce operational inefficiency, and support better customer experience.
